Alice Martinez — Game Portfolio

Zelda Dungeon
A remake of the first dungeon from The Legend of Zelda.
I worked on enemy movement and attack behaviors using raycasting. I built a shared health system that both the player and enemies pulled from, which helped make damage feel consistent. I also set up enemy boundaries so they'd stay in their assigned rooms, and used an eventbus system to manage item pickups and how they affect gameplay.
▶ Launch
Flower Zen
A peaceful flower shop simulator with cozy vibes.
I programmed the full order-taking and scoring system from scratch. I also built the core gameplay loop — letting players cut and arrange flowers to match requests. Most of my time went into making everything feel snappy and responsive, from clicking tools to completing orders.
▶ Launch
Lolie The Roly-Poly
A puzzle-platformer where a tiny bug journeys underground to reunite with her family.
I designed and animated bugs like ants, worms, and spiders — giving them behaviors that respond to the player (like spiders waiting to strike from webs). I also worked on the level layout, the underground visual environment, and all the scene transitions like fade-ins and fade-outs.
